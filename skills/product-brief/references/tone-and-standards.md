# Tone & standards for product briefs

> No standalone Xplor standards doc existed when this was written — these are derived from the
> *(PES) Nutrition Menus* brief (page `15004368928`) and should be reviewed and adjusted by the
> product team. Treat as a living draft.

## Voice
- **Concise and evidence-led.** Short paragraphs. Lead with the point, then support it.
- **Quantify wherever possible.** "237 votes since 2019", "reduce average time to create a
  nutrition event" — numbers beat adjectives.
- **Customer-first framing.** State the customer problem in their terms before any solution.
- **Plain language.** Write for an engineer, a designer, and a stakeholder to all understand it.

## Quality bar (the succinctness contract)
- Every requirement traces to research, a customer signal, or a logged decision. No orphan
  requirements.
- Success metrics are **measurable** and tied to the hypothesis.
- Acceptance criteria are **Given / When / Then** and testable.
- The **decision log is kept alive** — record what was decided, why, when, and who was present;
  strike through (don't delete) rescinded decisions so the history stays legible.
- **Out of Scope is explicit.** Naming what you won't do prevents scope creep and sets expectations.
- Open questions have **owners** and dates; an unowned question is a risk.

## What "good" looks like
- A reader gets the gist from Background + Opportunity Assessment alone.
- Cross-functional impact is honestly assessed (which surfaces, what effort) — not glossed.
- Risks are categorised (Value / Usability / Feasibility / Viability) with real mitigations.
- The brief links out to its research report and discovery initiative, so the provenance is one
  click away.

## What to avoid
- Restating the research report instead of citing it.
- Solutioning in the Background.
- Vague metrics ("improve experience") or unfalsifiable hypotheses.
- Inventing requirements to fill a section — leave a flagged placeholder instead.
