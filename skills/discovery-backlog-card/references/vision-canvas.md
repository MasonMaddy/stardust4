# Product Vision Canvas

A one-page frame for thinking through an opportunity *before* committing to a solution. Fill it at
**outcome level** — what changes and why, not how you'll build it. It's the thinking structure
behind the discovery card: each dimension distils to a 1–2 line field on the card.

Use **one canvas per opportunity**. If you can't answer a box, that's a discovery question — put it
in the card's **Key unknowns** with an owner.

## The six dimensions

**Vision** *(top — the headline)*
> What will change when this campaign, feature or product is released?

The future state in a sentence or two. Outcome, not feature list.

**Customer / User problems** *(left)*
> What product-agnostic problems are you solving?
> What will be better in customers' lives when they have used our product / service?

The problem as the customer experiences it — independent of any particular solution.

**Business opportunity / problem** *(right)*
> How will the company benefit?
> What are the business goals?

Why Xplor should care: the commercial or strategic upside.

**Hypotheses** *(left)*
> What do we believe is preventing the vision from being a reality?
> What do we believe will solve the customer problems?
> Start with "I believe that…"

Beliefs, clearly labelled as beliefs — these are what discovery sets out to test.

**Feature constraints** *(right)*
> e.g. time, budget, regulatory environment, stakeholder availability.

What limits or shapes the solution space.

**Success metrics** *(bottom — full width)*
> How will we know we have succeeded? What measurements will prove it?

Don't free-text this — frame it with **Pirate Metrics (AARRR)**. See `pirate-metrics.md`. (The STDC
and HEART frameworks are valid alternatives; AARRR is the default here.)

## Canvas → discovery card mapping

| Vision Canvas box | Discovery card field |
|---|---|
| Vision | **Vision** |
| Customer / User problems | **Customer / user problem** (+ informs *Who is affected*) |
| Business opportunity / problem | **Business opportunity** |
| Hypotheses | **Hypotheses** ("We believe that…") |
| Feature constraints | **Constraints & dependencies** |
| Success metrics | **Success metrics (Pirate / AARRR)** table |

## Guidance

- **Outcome over solution.** "Educators spend less time on compliance admin," not "add a bulk-edit
  button."
- **Evidence stays in the report.** The canvas is a frame for judgement; quotes, counts, and
  sources live in the linked research report — the card just points to them.
- **Unknowns are allowed.** A blank box is a signal, not a failure — convert it to an owned
  discovery question.
