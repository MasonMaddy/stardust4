# Discovery backlog card template (Jira)

At Xplor a "discovery backlog card" is a Jira **Initiative** in the **`XR` (Xplor Roadmap)**
project, set to status **In discovery**. It holds an opportunity whose research/discovery is not
yet complete, and links out to its Confluence brief once one exists. Model: **XR-83**.

Created from `product-research` when scope is research-incomplete. Confirm the project key + issue
type via `atlassian-write.md` before any write, and follow the draft→approve→write gate.

## Structure (from the XR Initiative model)

- **Summary** — the opportunity in one line.
- **Problem** — what's broken/needed today and the cost of it.
- **Vision** — the intended future state (still outcome-level, light on solution detail).
- **Who is affected** — table: Primary · Secondary · Future · Out of scope.
- **Proposed approach** — early thinking, broken down by surface/workstream where useful.
- **Success metrics** — how we'll know it worked.
- **Key unknowns / discovery questions** — the open questions, **each with an owner**. This is the
  discovery checklist; it maps directly from the research report's open-questions section.
- **Known constraints & dependencies** — what limits or blocks this.
- **Out of scope (this phase)** — explicit exclusions.
- **Effort signal** — rough size / relative cost, if known.
- **Confluence brief** — link to the brief once `product-brief` produces one (empty while in
  discovery).

## Lifecycle
1. Created in `XR` (Initiative, *In discovery*) from `product-research`.
2. Filled out over time — re-run the skill to append findings, flow charts, maps, interview notes;
   answer the key unknowns and assign owners.
3. When the discovery questions are answered → hand off to `product-brief`, which produces the
   Confluence brief and links it here.

## Cross-linking
Always link the initiative ↔ the research report (Confluence) so they find each other, and later
the initiative ↔ the brief.
