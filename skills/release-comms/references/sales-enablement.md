# Step ④ — Sales Enablement (one-pager + objections)

## What it's for
Give the sales team what they need to position and sell the feature: what it is,
who it's for, the pitch, what's included by plan, and how to handle the
predictable objections. **One one-pager per feature** — specific to a single
feature, even when the release bundles several.

## What it needs
The confirmed Release Fact Sheet. Brand voice from `xplor-brand-guidelines`.

## What it gives back
A one-pager: positioning, the one-line pitch, target customer, value points,
what's included by plan/tier, and an objection-handling table.

## How to draft
1. Translate capabilities into customer value ("so that…"), not feature lists.
2. State who this is for and the single strongest pitch line.
3. Tie availability to plans/tiers explicitly — sales need to know what's
   gated.
4. Anticipate objections (price, complexity, "we already do this another way")
   and give a grounded response to each. Don't invent competitive claims; if a
   comparison isn't sourced, leave it out.

## Done when
- One-line pitch present and benefit-led.
- Value points map to real capabilities in the Fact Sheet.
- "Included by plan" is explicit and matches the Fact Sheet.
- At least 3 likely objections with responses.
- No unsourced competitive or third-party claims.

## Not its job
How-to instructions (③) or internal-only roadmap. Don't promise future
capabilities unless the Fact Sheet sources them.

## Template
```markdown
# Sales one-pager — <feature>

**The pitch (one line):** <benefit-led hook>
**Who it's for:** <segment>

## Why it matters
- <value point — capability "so that" outcome>
- <value point>

## What's included
- <by plan/tier, scoped by region if different>

## Objection handling
| They say | You say |
|---|---|
| <objection> | <grounded response> |
| <objection> | <grounded response> |
| <objection> | <grounded response> |
```

## Golden example
Value points read as outcomes; "included by plan" matches the Fact Sheet
exactly; objections include the real one sales hits, answered without
overclaiming.

## Adversarial cases
- **No competitive data in inputs:** omit competitor comparisons rather than
  inventing them.
- **Feature gated to a high tier:** make the gating unmissable so sales don't
  over-promise to lower-tier accounts.
