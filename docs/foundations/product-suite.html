Foundations

Product Suite

The Xplor Education products Stardust serves — what each surface is for, who uses it, and how they connect. Pair this with the Personas to name who a piece of work is for before designing it.

Two spaces

The suite splits into Business Management Software (BMS) — where childcare services are administered — and People Engagement Software (PES) — where educators and families live. Admins run the business in Office; educators document and log compliance in Playground; families book, pay, and stay connected in Home.

The surfaces

Office

BMS · web · service & provider admins

The admin surface: enrolments, bookings, fees, rooms, reporting, and family and child records. Used at two altitudes — a service admin running one service day to day, and a provider admin configuring and reporting across many. Most Office features need both views considered.

Playground Web

PES · web · educators & practice leads

The educator's proactive space: learning documentation against the EYLF, programming and planning, and reporting. Educators sit down and spend time here — depth and richness are appropriate.

Playground App

PES · iOS/Android · educators · shared in-room device

The educator's reactive space: compliance tasks first — room sign-in/out, nappy checks, sleep checks, headcounts. It runs on a shared device that belongs to the room and is passed between educators, so the design goal is minimum time-to-done: act, put the iPad down, get back to the children.

Home

PES · iOS/Android · parents, guardians & authorised contacts

The family surface: bookings first, then messages and notices, learning documentation, health data, payments, and statements. Used by parents and the ring around them — grandparents and authorised pick-up contacts with delegated access. Parent word-of-mouth drives service adoption, so the parent experience is a first-class outcome.

Hub

Kiosk · iPad in the service entryway

Self-serve child sign-in/out at drop-off — QR code or manual entry, no staff member needed. Used by whoever is dropping off, including someone seeing it for the first time, so it must have a zero learning curve. Configured and managed by admins in Office.

The customer journey

Waitlist → Enrolment → Attendance → Bookings Payments Compliance Learning docs

Families join a waitlist, enrol, and attend — long day care, outside-school-hours care, or vacation care. Admins own intake and management in Office; educators own the day-to-day in Playground; families book, pay, and receive everything the service shares in Home. A feature usually cuts across surfaces: an enrolment form is configured by a provider admin, completed by a parent, and its output shapes what educators see.

Integrations

QikKids (Australia) and Discover (New Zealand) are business-management systems in their own right that feed data down into Playground and Home. No design work happens inside them — but anything built for PES may be consuming data they originated, so designs should check what happens when a field they don't supply is missing, and whether NZ services behave differently.
