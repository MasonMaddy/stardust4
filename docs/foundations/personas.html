Personas

The people Xplor Education's products are designed for. These are composite research personas — representative, not real individuals — used to keep decisions anchored to genuine needs as work moves through the product development lifecycle.

How to use them

Reach for a persona when a decision needs a point of view: who is this for, what are they trying to do, and what gets in their way? They are most useful in Discovery and Concept & Design — naming the audience in a user story, pressure-testing a flow, or sense-checking a prototype.

ℹ️
Composite, not real. Names, photos, and quotes are illustrative stand-ins drawn from research themes — not identifiable customers. Treat them as a shared shorthand for real needs, and keep talking to actual customers.

People Engagement Software

Seven personas spanning the two sides of People Engagement Software — the educators and practice leads who run a centre, and the families who engage with it.

Angela Smith

Pedagogy & Practice Lead · Gold Coast, QLD

Thirty years in education and an expert in pedagogy. She wants to mentor educators and build curriculums, but struggles to track programming and planning across the provider and to collaborate with peers.

Claire Yu

Educator (Diploma of ECEC) · Geelong, VIC

An early-career educator keen to learn and embrace new tools. She wants to make the most of technology but finds it intimidating without clear guidance, and wants less time lost to admin.

Emily

Educator (Diploma of ECEC) · Hobart, TAS

A creative educator who loves working hands-on with children. Teaching is her outlet for creativity, and she's wary of technology that gets in the way of it.

James

Experienced Parent · Sydney, NSW

An experienced, stay-at-home parent who values smooth processes and a little free time. He's time-poor and not especially tech-savvy, so anything fiddly is a barrier.

Riley

Neighbour, Friend & Parent · Sydney, NSW

A stay-at-home parent who helps the people around them get things done and wants to free up time. Often acting on someone else's behalf rather than as the primary decision-maker.

Jeff

Grandparent · Sydney, NSW

Retired, and helps the family with childcare. He wants reassurance his grandchild is safe and well cared for, but has low confidence with technology and needs things kept simple.

Martin

New Parent · Melbourne, VIC

A new parent who works in tech, understands childcare deeply, and is curious about how educators use technology. He gets frustrated when information doesn't line up and wants clear guidance on how things work.
